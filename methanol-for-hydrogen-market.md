<p><p>The Methanol for Hydrogen market primarily consists of two types: methanol reforming and methanol decomposition. Methanol reforming involves converting methanol into hydrogen and carbon dioxide using heat and catalysts, making it a key method for efficient hydrogen production. In contrast, methanol decomposition focuses on breaking down methanol into hydrogen and carbon without producing carbon dioxide, offering a more sustainable approach. Both processes aim to support hydrogen fuel technologies and contribute to cleaner energy solutions.</p></p>
