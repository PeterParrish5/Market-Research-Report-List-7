<p><ul><li>Hot Extrusion Food Processing</li><li>Cold Extrusion Food Processing</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Extruded food processing involves shaping food by forcing it through a mold under heat and pressure. Hot extrusion heats ingredients, allowing them to expand and cook simultaneously, resulting in products like snacks and breakfast cereals with a crispy texture. Cold extrusion, on the other hand, processes food without significant heat, preserving nutrients and producing denser items such as pasta and meat substitutes. Both methods cater to different consumer preferences and applications in the food industry, enhancing texture and flavor.</p></p>
