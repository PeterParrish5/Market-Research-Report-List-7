<p><p>The anthrax vaccines market primarily consists of two types: live vaccines and cell-free protective antigen (PA) vaccines. Live vaccines utilize attenuated strains of Bacillus anthracis to stimulate an immune response, offering effective long-term immunity but posing risks of infection. In contrast, cell-free PA vaccines contain purified protective antigen components, ensuring safety and minimizing the risk of adverse reactions. Both types aim to prevent anthrax infection, targeting different populations, including military personnel, lab workers, and at-risk individuals.</p></p>
