<p><p>The automatic lathe market comprises two primary types: horizontal and vertical lathes. Horizontal lathes are designed for turning operations with the spindle axis parallel to the ground, allowing for efficient machining of long and cylindrical parts. They are commonly used for high-volume production. Vertical lathes, on the other hand, feature a spindle that is oriented vertically, enabling better access for heavy and large workpieces. This design enhances stability and precision, making vertical lathes ideal for complex machining tasks in various industries.</p></p>
