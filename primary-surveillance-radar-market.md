<p><p>Primary Surveillance Radar (PSR) is a type of radar system used primarily in air traffic control and defense to detect and track aircraft without relying on cooperative signals from the aircraft, such as transponders. It operates by emitting radio waves that bounce off objects, allowing it to determine the position, speed, and direction of airborne targets. PSR is critical for ensuring airspace safety, especially in areas where secondary radar systems may not be operational or reliable.</p><p>The Primary Surveillance Radar Market is experiencing significant growth, driven by increasing air traffic, modernization of air traffic management systems, and rising defense expenditures worldwide. The L-Band PSR operates at 1-2 GHz, providing longer range but lower resolution, ideal for wide-area surveillance.
