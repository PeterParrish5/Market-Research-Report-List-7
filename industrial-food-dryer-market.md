<p><p>The industrial food dryer market encompasses various types, each tailored for specific drying needs. Spray dryers atomize liquid foods into fine droplets, quickly removing moisture. Freeze dryers preserve food by sublimating ice under vacuum conditions. Fluidized-bed dryers use hot air to suspend particles, ensuring even drying. Drum dryers feature heated cylindrical surfaces for continuous drying. Vacuum dryers operate under reduced pressure to lower boiling points, while tray dryers utilize heated air circulated around stacked trays. Each type offers distinct advantages based on food characteristics and production requirements.</p></p>
