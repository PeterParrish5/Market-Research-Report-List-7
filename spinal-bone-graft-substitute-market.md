<p><p>The spinal bone graft substitute market comprises four main types: Allograft, which uses bone from human donors; Synthetic Bone Graft, made from materials like calcium phosphates or ceramics; Xenograft, derived from animal bones, typically bovine; and Autograft, where the patient’s own bone is harvested for grafting. Each type offers unique advantages in terms of biocompatibility, availability, and risk of rejection, catering to various surgical needs and patient conditions in spinal fusion and orthopedic procedures.</p></p>
