<p><p>The Overhead Catenary System (OCS) is a network of wires suspended above railway tracks to supply electric power to trains, trams, or trolleybuses. <p><p>The Overhead Catenary System (OCS) market includes two main types: Flexible and Rigid systems. The **Flexible Overhead Catenary System** uses suspended wires and tensioning mechanisms, allowing adaptability to varying terrain and speeds, commonly used in railways and trams. The **Rigid Overhead Catenary System** employs fixed, solid conductors, offering stability and reduced maintenance, ideal for urban transit and tunnels. Both systems provide electrification for rail networks, with flexibility and rigidity catering to different operational and environmental requirements.</p></p>
