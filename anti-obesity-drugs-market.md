<p><p>The anti-obesity drugs market is categorized into peripherally acting and centrally acting anti-obesity drugs. Peripherally acting drugs target receptors outside the central nervous system, largely influencing appetite regulation and fat absorption. These tend to reduce calorie intake and promote weight loss via gastrointestinal mechanisms. Conversely, centrally acting drugs act on the brain, aiming to alter neurotransmitter levels that control hunger and satiety, thereby reducing food intake and encouraging weight loss. Both categories aim to combat obesity effectively.</p></p>
