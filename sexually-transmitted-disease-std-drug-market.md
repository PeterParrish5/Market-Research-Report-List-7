<p><p>The Sexually Transmitted Disease (STD) drug market comprises three primary types: antibiotics, antivirals, and vaccines. Antibiotics are used to treat bacterial STDs like chlamydia and gonorrhea, effectively targeting and eliminating infections. Antivirals address viral STDs, such as HIV and herpes, managing symptoms and viral load but not curing the disease. Vaccines, like those for HPV and hepatitis B, provide preventative measures, helping reduce infection rates and associated health complications. Together, these segments play a crucial role in STD management and prevention.</p></p>
