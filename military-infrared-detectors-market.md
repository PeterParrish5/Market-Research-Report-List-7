<p><p>The military infrared detectors market is primarily categorized into uncooled and cooled infrared detectors. Uncooled infrared detectors operate at ambient temperatures and are lighter, more compact, and require less power, making them suitable for portable applications. In contrast, cooled infrared detectors function at lower temperatures, enhancing sensitivity and image quality, ideal for long-range surveillance and precision targeting. Both types serve critical roles in military operations, including reconnaissance, missile guidance, and threat detection, due to their ability to detect invisible heat signatures.</p></p>
