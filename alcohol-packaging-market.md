<p><p>The alcohol packaging market encompasses two key types: primary packaging and secondary packaging. Primary packaging refers to the direct container holding the beverage, such as bottles, cans, or kegs, designed for consumer use and preservation. Secondary packaging serves a supportive role, encompassing boxes or cartons used for grouping multiple primary packages for distribution, branding, and protection during transportation. Both packaging types play crucial roles in ensuring product integrity, enhancing consumer appeal, and facilitating logistics in the alcohol industry.</p></p>
