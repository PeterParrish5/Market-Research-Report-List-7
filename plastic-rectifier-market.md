<p><ul><li>Maximum Reverse Current</li><li>Maximum Peak Current</li><li>Maximum Reverse Voltage</li><li>Forward Voltage</li><li>Maximum Average Rectified Current</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The plastic rectifier market is characterized by various types based on performance specifications. Maximum Reverse Current indicates the highest current the rectifier can withstand in reverse without damage. Maximum Peak Current refers to the maximum instantaneous current it can handle. Maximum Reverse Voltage is the highest voltage that can be applied in the reverse direction. Forward Voltage is the voltage drop across the rectifier in the forward direction. Maximum Average Rectified Current denotes the average output current over a specific period. Each parameter is crucial for applications in electronic circuits.</p></p>
