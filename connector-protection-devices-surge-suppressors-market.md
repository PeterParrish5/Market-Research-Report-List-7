<p><p>Connector Protection Devices, specifically surge suppressors, are categorized into Type I and Type II markets based on their application and installation scenarios. Type I devices are designed for primary protection, installed at the service entrance to safeguard electrical systems against external surges like lightning strikes. In contrast, Type II devices provide secondary protection, typically placed at the equipment level to shield sensitive electronics from residual surges. This division addresses varying surge protection needs across residential, commercial, and industrial applications.</p></p>
