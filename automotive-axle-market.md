<p><p>The automotive axle market is categorized into front and rear axles, each serving distinct functions. Front axles, located at the front of vehicles, primarily support steering and absorb road shocks, commonly found in front-wheel-drive systems. Rear axles, positioned at the back, transmit power to the wheels in rear-wheel-drive or all-wheel-drive vehicles, ensuring propulsion and stability. Both types are integral to vehicle performance, with front axles enhancing maneuverability and rear axles optimizing traction and load-bearing capacity, catering to diverse automotive needs.</p></p>
