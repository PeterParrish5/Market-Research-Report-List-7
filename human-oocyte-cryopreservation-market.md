<p><strong>The Human Oocyte Cryopreservation Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Slow Freezing</li><li>Rapid Freezing</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Human oocyte cryopreservation involves two primary methods: slow freezing and rapid freezing. Slow freezing gradually decreases the temperature of oocytes, allowing for water removal and minimizing ice crystal formation, which can damage cells. In contrast, rapid freezing, or vitrification, involves an ultra-fast cooling process that transforms the oocytes into a glass-like state, preventing ice crystal formation. Both methods aim to preserve oocytes for future use, but variations in technique can influence the viability and success rates of thawed oocytes in assisted reproductive technologies.</p></p>
